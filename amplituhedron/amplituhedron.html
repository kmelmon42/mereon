Amplituhedron

The geometry underlying particle scattering

The simplest amplituhedron (k=1) is a cyclic polytope — the convex hull of n points on a moment curve in projective space. Its volume encodes the tree-level scattering amplitude for n gluons. What you see is a 3D projection of this higher-dimensional jewel.
