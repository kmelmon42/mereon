Introduction

What is a dipolygonid? A dipolygonid is the systematic generalization of Buckminster Fuller's Jitterbug. Start with a dipolygon: two regular polygons {m} and {n} sharing a common vertex P, with their planes meeting at dihedral angle θ. Replicate under finite rotation group G to get a symmetric structure capable of continuous uniform motion — each polygon simultaneously translates and rotates along its circumscribed cylinder axis — while preserving all symmetries.

Notation: (g/s){m} + (g/t){n} | θ where g = |G|, s and t are generator orders.

Verheyen's result: 2 infinite dihedral classes + 20 finite types (2 tetrahedral · 4 octahedral · 14 icosahedral).

About the 3D views: T₂ (the Jitterbug) uses exact geometry derived from the paper and verified against the octahedron→cuboctahedron transformation. All other types currently show schematic representations — polygon counts and symmetry groups are correct from the paper, but precise vertex coordinates have not yet been derived.

Dihedral Class A — Petrie Zigzags

n{2} + n{2} | (k/n)·180° Non-planar polygonal zigzag lines (Petrie polygons) in D_n. At central positions all digon centers collapse onto the n-fold axis.

Dihedral Class B — Prisms

n{2} + 2{n/k} | 90° Extreme positions are rectangular prisms.

Tetrahedral Group A₄

A₄ has order 12: 3 two-fold and 4 three-fold axes. T₂ lower half is Fuller's original Jitterbug — exact geometry verified.

Octahedral Group S₄

S₄ has order 24: 6 two-fold, 4 three-fold, 3 four-fold axes. O₄ is the only regular octahedral dipolygonid.

Icosahedral A₅ — I₁ through I₄

A₅ has order 60: 15 two-fold, 10 three-fold, 6 five-fold axes. I₄ is a regular pentagon type.

Icosahedral A₅ — I₅ through I₈

Mixed families including pentagram {5/2} star polygons. I₈ is regular.

Icosahedral A₅ — I₉ through I₁₁

Pentagon and pentagram combinations. I₁₁ is the unique equiradial non-regular type.

Icosahedral A₅ — I₁₂ through I₁₄

Triangle-heavy types. I₁₄ "Vampire" (20{3}+20{3}) named by Magnus Wenninger in 1979.

Regular Dipolygonids

Five types with m = n and R_A = R_B (both families congruent and equiradial).

Equiradial Dipolygonid

R_A = R_B with m ≠ n — unique in the finite classification.

Notable Chiral Pairs

Structures not self-enantiomorphous, existing as dextro/laevo pairs.
